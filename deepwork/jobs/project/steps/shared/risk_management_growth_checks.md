# Risk management growth and mission-scaling checks

Use this guidance when a project's ability to grow and fulfill its mission is
unclear from repo, notes, issue, or website evidence.

The point is not to force startup boilerplate. The point is to check whether the
project has the non-engineering systems required to turn product work into real
mission progress.

## Universal risk areas to inspect

These are common cross-functional failure modes that block projects from growing
to meet their mission:

1. **No clear growth plan**
   - The team cannot explain how awareness, demand, adoption, or usage should
     increase over time.

2. **Weak audience-building and distribution**
   - No repeatable channels for reaching the intended audience.
   - No social presence, content loop, partnerships, launch cadence, or other
     discoverability mechanism.

3. **No customer-interaction surface**
   - Users have no obvious place to ask questions, give feedback, report pain,
     or form community.
   - Examples: missing GitHub Discussions, Discord, forum, support/contact path,
     office hours, or demo intake flow.

4. **Weak customer discovery**
   - The project lacks interviews, evaluator notes, support logs, usability
     feedback, or other direct evidence of user needs and objections.

5. **Unclear ICP or problem definition**
   - The project is trying to serve everyone, or the problem statement is too
     vague to guide positioning and prioritization.

6. **Weak onboarding and activation**
   - Users may discover the project, but the first-success path is unclear,
     fragile, undocumented, or unsupported.

7. **Weak retention or repeat-use loop**
   - The project does not know what makes users come back, expand usage, upgrade,
     or keep integrating the product into their workflow.

8. **Thin KPI and measurement discipline**
   - No scorecard for demand, activation, retention, conversion, release health,
     or mission progress.

9. **No credible path to profitability or sustainable funding**
   - The project does not know how it funds continued work, how it becomes
     financially sustainable, or whether adoption creates leverage instead of
     an expanding support burden.

10. **Delivery focus disconnected from mission**
    - The team is shipping a lot, but the work is not clearly compounding toward
      mission success, adoption, revenue, trust, or strategic leverage.

11. **Trust, credibility, or compliance gaps**
    - The mission depends on user trust, safety, reliability, or compliance, but
      the project has weak proof, documentation, or assurance mechanisms.

12. **Decision-making and operating-rhythm weakness**
    - No regular review of goals, KPIs, tradeoffs, blockers, or reprioritization.
    - The project reacts to incoming work instead of steering.

## Questions to ask when evidence is thin

Ask a short batch of targeted clarification questions before finalizing the
assessment when the answers materially affect the diagnosis:

1. Does the project have a deliberate growth plan yet, or is growth work still
   undefined?
2. What are the primary acquisition or audience-building channels today?
3. Which social channels matter for this project, and are any currently active?
4. Is YouTube, video demos, or educational content part of the plan?
5. Where can customers or evaluators interact with the project today:
   GitHub Discussions, Discord, forum, email/contact form, demo calls, or some
   other channel?
6. Who is the primary customer or evaluator, and what exact problem is the
   project trying to solve for them?
7. What is the first-success moment, and where does onboarding currently fail?
8. What signals indicate repeat use, expansion, or retention?
9. What metrics or scorecards are used to judge mission progress today?
10. Is there a credible path to profitability or sustainable funding, and are
    there pricing, staffing, trust, compliance, or operational constraints that
    could block growth even if demand increases?
11. If these systems are missing, is that intentional or an acknowledged gap?

## Evidence to check before asking

- repo README and docs
- website navigation and footer
- release notes, blog posts, newsletters, and social drafts
- issue tracker labels and milestones
- notes hub fields and related literature/report notes
- onboarding docs, support docs, FAQ, and contact surfaces
- KPI dashboards, charter notes, milestone goals, or success reviews
- pricing pages, plans, sales/contact pages, sponsorship or funding notes, and
  any evidence of a path to profitability or durable financial support
- compliance, security, reliability, or trust documentation when relevant

## How to treat missing systems

- Do not assume missing distribution, onboarding, community, measurement, or
  trust systems are harmless.
- Treat missing social presence, missing YouTube or demo content, missing
  customer-interaction surfaces, missing onboarding evidence, missing retention
  signals, missing KPI discipline, or no credible path to profitability or
  sustainable funding as first-class business risks when the project appears to
  need public distribution, user education, or feedback loops.
- If the user does not answer and no durable evidence exists, record the gap as
  an explicit unknown or risk rather than smoothing it over.

## Prioritization lens

When these risks are present, prefer diagnosing whether the project is blocked
by:

- **demand generation** problems
- **activation/onboarding** problems
- **retention/repeat-use** problems
- **business model, profitability, or resourcing** problems
- **trust/compliance** problems
- **focus and operating-rhythm** problems

This helps the workflow recommend what should change now instead of collapsing
all non-engineering issues into vague "marketing" advice.
